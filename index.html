Jack Churchill

"Mad Jack" Churchill

"The Man Who Fought in WWII with a Sword and Bow"

John Malcolm Thorpe Fleming Churchill was a British Army officer that fought in the Second World War with a longbow, a basket-hilted Scottish broadsword, and a set of bagpipes.


"Any officer who goes into action without his sword is improperly dressed”.


Early life

1906 Born in Colombo, British Ceylon

1924 Played a small role in the film The Thief of Bagdad due to his talent in Archery and in playing the bagpipes.

1926 Graduated from the Royal Military College, Sandhurst and served in Burma with the Manchester Regiment.

1938 Appeared in yet another film: A Yank at Oxford. Took second place in the military piping competition at the Aldershot Tattoo.

1939 Got 26th place at the World Archery Championships in Oslo while representing Great Britain. After the Nazi Germany invaded Poland he resumed his commission and was assigned to the Manchester Regiment, which was sent to France.

Second World War - France

1940 Alongside some of his man Churchill ambushed a German patrol near L'Épinette giving the attack signal by raising his broadsword.

Second World War - Norway

1941 During a raid on the German garrison at Vågsøy, Norway, Churchill sprang into action as the landing ramps dropped, playing "March of the Cameron Men" on his bagpipes. He then hurled a grenade and stormed into the fight. For his bravery at both Dunkirk and Vågsøy, Churchill was awarded the Military Cross.

Second World War - Italy

1943 Churchill led No. 2 Commando during the Catania landings, equipped with his signature Scottish broadsword, a longbow and arrows, and bagpipes. He captured a German observation post near Molina, taking 42 prisoners. For his leadership in this action at Salerno, he was awarded the Distinguished Service Order.

Second World War - Yugoslavia

1944 Churchill led Commandos in Yugoslavia to support Tito's Partisans from the island of Vis. During a raid on the German-occupied island of Brač, he led 40 Commando in a flanking maneuver. Only he and six others reached the target. A mortar shell incapacitated everyone, and as the Germans advanced, Churchill played "Will Ye No Come Back Again?" on his bagpipes. He was captured, taken to Berlin for interrogation, and then transferred to a special compound at Sachsenhausen concentration camp.

Second World War - Capture

1945 Churchill, along with around 140 notable concentration camp inmates, was moved to Tyrol under SS guard. Fearing for their lives, the prisoners approached German army officers. Captain Wichard von Alvensleben’s unit intervened, causing the SS guards to withdraw. The prisoners were freed, and Churchill then walked 150 kilometers to Verona, Italy, where he met an American armored unit.

Second World War - Burma

1945As the Pacific War was still on, Churchill was sent to Burma where some of the largest land battles against Japan were being fought. By the time Churchill reached India, Hiroshima and Nagasaki had been bombed, and the war ended. Churchill was said to be unhappy with the sudden end of the war: "If it wasn't for those damn Yanks, we could have kept the war going another 10 years!"

Postwar

1945After World War II, Churchill became a certified parachutist and transferred to the Seaforth Highlanders. He was soon stationed in Mandatory Palestine as the executive officer of the 1st Battalion, Highland Light Infantry.

1948 In the spring, just before the British mandate ended, Churchill tried to help the Hadassah medical convoy, which was attacked by Arab forces. Ignoring orders to avoid the conflict, he offered to use an APC to evacuate the convoy but was declined. He and his men provided covering fire, but 77 of the 79 convoy members were killed, an event now known as the Hadassah medical convoy massacre. Following this, Churchill organized the evacuation of 700 Jewish doctors, students, and patients from the Hadassah hospital on Mount Scopus, Jerusalem.

1959Churchill retired from the army.

Death

1996Churchill passed away on March 8, at the age of 89 in Surrey.

2014In March, the Royal Norwegian Explorers Club included Churchill in a book, recognizing him as one of the greatest explorers and adventurers of all time.
